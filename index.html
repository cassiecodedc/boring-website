Arlington: America's Fittest City—But Is It for Everyone?

By Cassie Huang

When I moved to Arlington, VA, from East Lansing, Michigan, I was thrilled to escape the cold weather. But what truly astonished me was how people here are always on the move. Quite literally.

Walking, strolling, jogging, running, hiking, even kayaking (that’s another story)—residents in Arlington seem to spend most of their time on their feet. This vibrant, active lifestyle was a surprising change from what I was used to.

Apparently, Arlington has ranked as the fittest city in America for six consecutive years, with Washington, DC, following closely. Granted, the area boasts an abundance of parks, trails, and wide sidewalks to stay active.

Upon researching and socializing, I discovered a group of friends who play badminton. However, it usually takes me an hour and a half just to get to the badminton court.

I often wonder why I have to drive so far to play a sport with my friends, but it seems there aren’t many alternatives. My research revealed a surprising scarcity of smaller facilities, especially compared to the vast networks of large gym monopolies.

Here is the map of DMV. DMV is an abbreviation for the Washington metropolitan area, which includes the District of Columbia, Maryland, and Virginia (DC, MD, and VA). The area is also known as the National Capital Region.

There are 38 Onelife Fitness in the DMV area.

There are 84 Anytime Fitness in the DMV area.

There are 112 Planet Fitness in the DMV area.

The number of gyms in the DMV area is truly staggering.

Within a 20-minute drive from where I live, there are eight gyms to choose from.

Yet, I still have to drive 1 hour and a half just to play badminton with my friends.
